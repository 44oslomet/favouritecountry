Let's tell you a bit about Peru

Peru is a country on the West coast of South America. It’s known for its diverse landscape, offering explorers the joy of steep mountains, dry deserts, lush rainforests and picture perfect beaches. With a rich culture and history, dating all the way back to the Inca Empire, Peru never gets boring and keeps you wanting back long after your visit.

Quick facts

  • Lima is the capital
  • The official language is Spanish
  • It has a population of 33 million people
  • The currency is Sol

What you can experience

Machu Picchu
Set in the Andes Mountains, this Incan citadel is a hell of sight. Built in the 1500th century, before abandoned, it offers it’s visitors a travel back in time where sophisticated dry-stone walls and panoramic views was everyday life for those who lived there. Today Machu Picchu is a part of the UNESCO World Heritage List, a must-see wonder ready to be crossed off your bucket list.
Paracas
A city by the coast, known for its beautiful sunsets, rich sea life and the Paracas desert meeting the South Pacific Ocean. Most tourists visit this part of Peru to experience the wild life that Islas Ballestas offer. Here you’ll see penguins and sea lions, alongside Inca terns, pelicans and dolphins. Back at the mainland you’ll be merged with the coasts laidback and peaceful lifestyle.
Arequipa
Framed by three vulcanos, Arequipa is a city that never sleeps. The colonial-era is filled with baroque buildings, creating a historical charm. This is also a popular starting point for those who wish to visit the Colca Canyon, the world’s second deepest canyon. It offers some incredible tracking experiences. Make sure you don't miss out!
Lima
Lima, Peru’s super hip capital. If you’re a food lover this is a place you’ll find indulging. The flavours you’ll experience are not only based on history, but are also of multi-cultural influence. This is the best place to eat the Peruvian signature dish, ceviche. Alongside the food scene, Lima is known for it's boheiman sub-culture and great street art. Enjoy!
